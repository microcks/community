# Microcks Steering Committee Charter

## Purpose

The goal of the **Microcks Steering Committee (SC)** is to ensure that Microcks
meets the needs of its current and future users.

The Steering Committee represents the voice of the community, particularly [adopters](https://github.com/microcks/.github/blob/main/ADOPTERS.md) and production users and works alongside maintainers to guide development efforts toward solving concrete, real-world problems.

The Steering Committee complements the maintainers' and code owners technical leadership by providing strategic direction, governance clarity, and community alignment.

## Objectives

The Steering Committee aims to:

1. Improve overall project visibility.
2. Strengthen governance clarity.
3. Increase contributions and community involvement.
4. Encourage diversity across the Microcks community.
5. Provide strategic guidance aligned with CNCF and project expectations.
6. Support the long-term sustainability and continuity of the project.

## Steering Committee Responsibilities

The Steering Committee’s responsibilities include:

1. Providing feedback to maintainers and code owners about Microcks' feature set, UX, APIs, integrations, and operational model.
2. Assisting maintainers in prioritizing roadmap items and upcoming work.
3. Representing the "voice of the Microcks user" internally and externally.
4. Offering neutral mediation for non-technical disputes when required.
5. Developing and maintaining a project continuity and sustainability plan.
6. Supporting CNCF alignment, incubation/graduation processes, and governance maturity.
7. Participating in Code of Conduct discussions if a violation is reported.

The Steering Committee does **not** override technical decisions made by
maintainers or code owners but may provide strategic recommendations.

## Steering Committee Membership

The Steering Committee comprises a limited number of members to ensure effective discussion and decision-making.

At this time, no fixed upper limit is imposed. The project values inclusivity and aims to onboard all individuals who demonstrate sustained and active commitment to the Microcks community.

Should the size of the Steering Committee grow to a point where its effectiveness, agility, or governance clarity is impacted, the Committee may introduce a formal upper bound through an amendment to this charter.

### Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for membership, a candidate must:

1. Be an active user, adopter, contributor, or ecosystem partner of Microcks.
2. Be responsible for or involved in a non-trivial Microcks deployment, contribution,
   or integration.
3. Be willing and able to attend regularly scheduled Steering Committee meetings.
4. Abide by the [Microcks Code of Conduct](https://github.com/microcks/.github/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

### Nomination & Selection

- Candidates may be nominated by:
  - Current Steering Committee members, or
  - Microcks maintainers/code owners.
- A candidate must have at least **two nominators** from this group.

### Appointment Process

If 3 or fewer eligible candidates are nominated for available seats, the nominees shall assume their seats without requiring a formal vote.

In the event that 3 Steering Committee members are already seated and additional nominations are received, the existing Steering Committee members shall evaluate and select new members using a simple ranked voting process.

In case of a tie:

- The tie shall be resolved by a vote among maintainers.
- If still tied, a fair and transparent random selection process will be used.

### Term Duration

- Membership duration is **one year**.
- Members may seek renewal following the same nomination process.
- Membership expires if eligibility conditions are no longer met.

## Steering Committee Meetings

Steering Committee meetings should align with the [project release process](https://github.com/microcks/microcks/blob/master/RELEASE.md), particularly the Design phase, to better support roadmap guidance.

- Meetings will occur **at least once per quarter**.
- Additional meetings may be scheduled as needed.
- Meetings are expected to be transparent.
- Minutes and recordings will be livestreamed or published publicly whenever possible.

Maintainers are expected to attend meetings to provide input and alignment.

## Decision-Making Process

The Steering Committee operates primarily by **consensus**.

If consensus cannot be reached:

- A simple majority vote among SC members applies.
- Certain governance changes may require approval from both:
  - A majority of Steering Committee members, and
  - A majority of maintainers.

## Changes to This Charter

Changes to this document must be approved by:

- A majority of Steering Committee members, and
- A majority of Microcks maintainers.

Exceptions:

- The list of Steering Committee members may be updated under the conditions defined in the Membership section.

## Relationship with Maintainers

- Maintainers retain authority over technical decisions.
- The Steering Committee provides strategic and governance guidance.
- Both groups are expected to collaborate transparently.

## Transparency & Community Engagement

The Steering Committee commits to:

- Acting in the best interest of the Microcks project.
- Maintaining open communication with the community.
- Ensuring governance decisions are documented.
- Supporting inclusivity and diversity within the ecosystem.

## Code of Conduct

All Steering Committee members must adhere to the Microcks Code of Conduct.

Violations may result in removal from the Steering Committee.

*This document formalizes the governance structure supporting the Microcks community and aligns with CNCF best practices for open source project maturity.*
